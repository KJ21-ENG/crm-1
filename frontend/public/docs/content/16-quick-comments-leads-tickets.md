# 16. Comment Dock
Status: completed
## 1) What this module is
The Comment Dock is a global, floating notification panel located at the bottom right of the screen. It keeps you connected to conversations, alerting you immediately whenever you are mentioned or receive a new message in a Lead or Ticket.

## 2) What staff can do here
- Instantly see when a teammate mentions them (`@name`) in a Lead or Ticket comment.
- Read new message updates without leaving their current screen.
- Send a quick reply directly from the notification panel.
- Mute or unmute specific conversation threads to control notification volume.
- Click a notification to jump directly to the specific Lead or Ticket record.

## 3) How to use (step-by-step)
1. Anytime you are mentioned or get a new message, the floating blue comment bubble will pulse with a red unread badge.
2. Click the floating blue bubble to open the Comment Dock.
![Screenshot](/docs/screenshots/16-quick-comments-leads-tickets/01-comment-dock-panel.png)
3. Review your recent messages. Unread messages have a solid blue dot.
4. To send a quick response, type your message in the "Reply..." box under a notification and click **Send**.
5. To stop receiving alerts for a specific conversation, click the **Bell** icon to mute the thread.
6. To view the full history, click the **Arrow** icon to open the original Lead or Ticket.

## 4) Important buttons/options
- **Red Notification Badge**: Shows the number of unread mentions or messages.
- **Quick Reply Input**: A text box to type and send a reply without navigating away.
- **Mute/Unmute (Bell icon)**: Toggles notifications off or on for a specific record.
- **Drill-down (Arrow icon)**: Takes you to the exact source document of the comment.
- **Mark as Read (Check icon)**: Clears the unread status of a single message.
- **Mark all as read (Top Check icon)**: Clears all new notification indicators at once.

## 5) Daily best practices
- Check the pulsing red badge frequently to stay responsive to team questions.
- Use the quick reply feature for short acknowledgments ("Done", "Checking now").
- Mute threads that are no longer relevant to your work to reduce noise.
- Use the Mark all as read button at the end of the day to start fresh tomorrow.

## 6) Common mistakes to avoid
- Leaving threads unmuted when you are no longer involved, leading to notification clutter.
- Reading a ping but forgetting to reply because it was cleared from the unread list.
- Sending a long, complex reply via Quick Reply instead of opening the full record where formatting is easier.
